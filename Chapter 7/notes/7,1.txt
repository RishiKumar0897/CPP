- compound statement is a group of zero or more statements that is treated by the compiler as if it were a single statement (also known as blocks)
- blocks are bracketed by {} and statements in between are to be executed
- no semicolon needed at the end of a blocks
- example of a block is a function body

- most common use case for blocks is if-else statements
- you can nest blocks inside of other blocks
- nesting level of a function is the max num of nested blocks you can be inside at any point in the function
- best practice is to keep nesting level of functions to 3 or less
