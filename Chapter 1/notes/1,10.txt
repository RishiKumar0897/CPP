- an expresison is a non-empty sequence of literals, variables, operators, and function calls that calculates a value
- process of executing an expression is called evaluation
- resulting value produced is the result of the expression (return value)
    - the resylt of an expression is either: a value, an object or a function, or nothing (void)


- when an expression is evaluated, each of the terms inside the expression are evaluated until a single value remains

- expressions are part of statements
- an expression statement is a statement that consists of an expression followed by a semicolon
    - when an expresison statement is executed, the expression will be evaluated


- a subexpression is an expression used as an operand
- a full expression is an expression that is not a subexpression
- a compound expression consists of two or more uses of operators.
